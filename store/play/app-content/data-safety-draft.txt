Google Play Data safety draft

Use this as the Play Console Data safety form source of truth. Review before
submission against the exact AAB and any third-party SDKs included in that build.

High-level answers:

- Does the app collect or share any required user data types?
  Yes.

- Is all user data encrypted in transit?
  Yes. Zaptec API calls use HTTPS.

- Can users request that data is deleted?
  Yes for hosted web app server-side data via the in-app Delete server data
  action. For native Android local data, users can clear app storage or uninstall
  the app.

- Is the app committed to following the Google Play Families Policy?
  No, because the app is not targeted to children.

Data collected:

1. Personal info / Email address

- Collected: Yes.
- Shared: Yes, with Zaptec for authentication.
- Purpose: App functionality and account management.
- Required: Yes, required to sign in and synchronize charging data.
- Processed ephemerally: No. The app stores the signed-in email locally as part
  of the app session/customer data.

2. App activity / App interactions

- Collected: Yes.
- Shared: No.
- Purpose: Analytics and app functionality.
- Required: No, analytics helps understand app usage and app preferences improve
  the experience.
- Examples: privacy-focused Firebase Analytics events such as login success or
  failure, demo login, sync completion or failure, filter category changes, and
  PDF export. Locally stored app preferences include filters, selected period,
  column setup, kWh price, and currency settings.

3. App info and performance / Diagnostics

- Collected: No.
- Shared: No.
- Notes: No crash reporting SDK is currently present in the project. Firebase
  Analytics is present for app interaction events, not crash diagnostics.

4. Financial info

- Collected: No.
- Shared: No.
- Notes: The app stores a user-entered kWh price and calculates estimated charge
  costs. This is not payment, banking, credit, investment, tax, insurance, or
  account-balance data.

5. Location

- Collected: No.
- Shared: No.
- Notes: No location permission is declared.

6. Device or other IDs

- Collected: Review in Play Console.
- Shared: Review in Play Console.
- Notes: Firebase Analytics may use an app instance identifier or other
  analytics-related identifiers depending on Firebase configuration and the
  uploaded Android build. Confirm the exact Play Data safety selection before
  submission.

Other data handled by the app:

- Zaptec password:
  Entered by the user and sent to Zaptec over HTTPS to request access. Charge
  Ledger does not store the Zaptec password.

- Zaptec access token:
  Used for app functionality. Native Android storage should be reviewed against
  the final release build before submission.

- Charger and charge session data:
  Synchronized from Zaptec and stored locally in the native Android app. Includes
  charger metadata and charge sessions such as start/end time, charger name,
  user name when supplied by Zaptec, energy, duration, and cost where available.

Data sharing:

- Zaptec receives login credentials and API requests so the app can authenticate
  and synchronize the user's charging data.
- Charge Ledger does not sell personal data.
- Charge Ledger does not share data for advertising or profiling.

Security practices:

- Data is encrypted in transit where sent off device.
- Native app data is stored locally on the Android device.
- The app does not request location, contacts, camera, microphone, SMS, calendar,
  phone, or storage/media permissions.
